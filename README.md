# XR Reading Usability Study (VR vs AR vs Web)

## Overview

This project explores reading experiences and the interpretation of privacy policies (EULAs) across three platforms:

* Virtual Reality (VR) – Meta Quest
* Augmented Reality (AR) – Microsoft HoloLens
* Traditional Web

The study focuses on usability, interaction challenges, and how users engage with digital policies in immersive environments.

---

## Research Goals

* Compare reading experiences across VR, AR, and web
* Evaluate usability challenges in XR environments
* Examine how users interpret privacy policies (EULAs)
* Measure cognitive workload using NASA-TLX

---

## Study Design

* Participants: Ages 8–11 (anonymized)
* Tasks:

  * Reading long-form content (stories)
  * Navigating and interpreting privacy policies
  * Answering comprehension questions

---

## Data Collection

* NASA Task Load Index (NASA-TLX)
* Qualtrics surveys
* Observational notes
* Post-task interviews

---

## Key Findings

* VR provided immersive interaction but caused discomfort (e.g., dizziness) for some users
* AR felt more natural but required learning gestures
* Web reading was easiest but less engaging
* Reading long documents like EULAs in XR presented usability challenges
* Participants showed curiosity about data privacy but struggled to interpret policy language

---

## Analysis

Statistical comparisons were performed using:

* Paired t-tests
* Wilcoxon signed-rank tests

See `data-analysis/` for code and results.

---

## Ethics & Privacy

All participant data has been anonymized.
No personal identifiers or sensitive information are included in this repository.

---

## Why This Matters

Understanding usability challenges in XR reading environments is important for designing accessible, user-friendly, and responsible immersive systems.
